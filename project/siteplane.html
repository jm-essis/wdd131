EcoTrails Explorer

WDD 131 Project Site Plan

1. Site Name

EcoTrails Explorer

Reason for selection: The name clearly conveys the purpose of the platform: exploring eco-friendly nature trails. It targets outdoor enthusiasts, hikers, and conservationists looking to discover paths while promoting minimum-impact ethics (Leave No Trace).

2. Site Purpose

The purpose of EcoTrails Explorer is to provide a reliable, responsive regional hub for hikers to discover local trails, check difficulty ratings, and dynamically filter paths based on their current needs. The site aims to build a community around responsible trekking by featuring an interactive trail log (powered by JavaScript array methods and local storage) and a volunteer cleanup/newsletter signup form.

3. Scenarios

The target audience includes casual walkers, hobbyist hikers, and eco-conscious travelers. Here are two primary questions visitors will look to answer:

  • Scenario 1: "I am looking for an easy, child-friendly hiking trail nearby that is under 3 miles long. How can I filter my options quickly without leaving the page?"
  • Scenario 2: "I want to track the trails I've completed this summer and sign up to volunteer for the upcoming community trail cleanup event. Where can I log my hikes and register?"

5. Typography

  • Primary Heading Font: 'Segoe UI' (or Helvetica/Arial fallback)

    Applied to all major <h1>, <h2>, and <h3> elements to provide a clean, modern, and easily readable structure.

  • Body Font: Tahoma (or sans-serif fallback)

    Applied to paragraphs, lists, forms, and table structures. Tahoma offers compact spacing and excellent legibility across small mobile breakpoints and large desktop screens.
